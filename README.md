📌 Project Overview
Coffee shops generate thousands of transactions daily, making it challenging for management to monitor sales performance, 
customer preferences, and operational efficiency. This project focuses on transforming raw transactional data into actionable 
business insights through an interactive Excel dashboard.
The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of sales performance, product demand, store-wise revenue distribution, and customer 
purchasing patterns, enabling data-driven decision-making.

🎯 Business Problem
Coffee sho management lacked visibility into key business metrics and needed answers to questions such as:
Which products generate the highest sales volume?
Which store locations perform best?
What are the peak sales hours?
Which product categories drive the majority of revenue?
What size preferences do customers have?
How can staffing and inventory decisions be optimized?
This dashboard was developed to address these challenges and support business growth through data analytics.

📈 Key Insights
🏆 Top Product Analysis:
Ethiopian, Our Old Time Diner Blend, Colombian, and Brazilian products were among the highest-selling products.
A small group of products contributed significantly to total sales volume.
📍 Location-wise Performance:
Sales were distributed almost equally across:
Lower Manhattan
Hell's Kitchen
Astoria
No single location dominated overall sales performance.
⏰ Hourly Sales Analysis:
Peak sales occurred during morning hours.
Sales gradually declined during later hours of the day.
Morning periods represent the highest customer traffic.
☕ Product Category Analysis:
Coffee and Tea generated the highest sales volume.
Bakery and Drinking Chocolate showed moderate demand.
Packaged products contributed minimally to overall sales.
🥤 Product Size Analysis:
Regular and Large sizes accounted for the majority of purchases.
Small-sized products represented the lowest share of sales.

💡 Business Recommendations
Inventory Optimization:
Maintain higher stock levels for top-selling coffee products.
Monitor low-performing categories to reduce excess inventory.
Workforce Planning:
Increase staffing during peak morning hours.
Optimize shift schedules based on hourly demand patterns.
Marketing Strategy:
Create promotional campaigns for underperforming categories.
Bundle popular products to increase average order value.
Location-Based Strategy:
Continue balanced inventory allocation across locations.
Develop location-specific promotions based on customer preferences.
